Load your data from a link

A way to skip the file-passing dance (and the OneDrive that keeps stopping). Here's how it would work — then you decide.

The difference

Today — 5 steps
1. I make a data file 2. It goes to OneDrive / chat 3. You find it on your phone 4. You download it 5. You import it in the app Breaks when OneDrive stops 😕
With a link — 1 tap
You tap “Sync now” in the app It pulls the latest data itself Done. No files, no OneDrive ✅

How it would work

Step 1
I prepare your data once
Same as now — I turn your statements into your portfolio data (Baraka, Sarwa, …).
Step 2
I host it at a private link
The data lives at one secret web address. I paste that link into your app once, and the app remembers it.
Step 3
When data changes, I refresh the link
New statement? I update what's at that same address. You don't touch any files.
Step 4
You tap “Sync now”
The app fetches the latest data from the link and updates instantly. One tap, done — no OneDrive, no downloads.

The honest trade-off

Your data would live at a web link

Right now your data only exists as a file you hold. With a link, the financial numbers sit at a secret web address so the app can fetch them. There's no name, date of birth, or location in it (your rule) — just amounts and holdings. But anyone who got that exact secret link could read those numbers. It's a small privacy trade for a lot less hassle.

Your options

Secret link (recommended) — an unlisted address only you and I know; I can change it anytime if you ever want to “revoke” it.
Password-locked — the data is scrambled and the app asks for a PIN to unlock it. More private, one extra step.
Keep files — do nothing; keep importing a file. Most private, but you deal with the OneDrive/file dance.
